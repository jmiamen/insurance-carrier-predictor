===METADATA===
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Medical Conditions and Medication Reference
PURPOSE: Agent reference guide for identifying health conditions from medications and mapping to carrier underwriting criteria
USE_CASE: Reverse lookup - client provides medications, agent identifies conditions and appropriate carrier positioning
VERSION: 1.0
LAST_UPDATED: 2025-11-11

===USAGE_INSTRUCTIONS===
HOW_TO_USE:
  1. Client provides list of current medications
  2. Search this file for medication names (generic or brand)
  3. Identify underlying health conditions
  4. Assess severity based on medication combinations
  5. Reference CARRIER_POSITIONING to match client to appropriate sales box
  6. Cross-reference with individual carrier underwriting files for final determination

SEVERITY_ASSESSMENT:
  SINGLE_MEDICATION: Often indicates well-controlled condition, may qualify for Box 1 or 2
  MULTIPLE_MEDICATIONS_SAME_CONDITION: Indicates more serious or poorly controlled condition, likely Box 3 or 4
  COMBINATION_CONDITIONS: Multiple conditions increase complexity, typically Box 3 or 4

===CONDITION_1_HEART_ATTACK===
CONDITION_NAME: Heart Attack (Post-Myocardial Infarction)
MEDICAL_TERM: Myocardial Infarction, MI, Acute Coronary Syndrome
SEVERITY: Major cardiac event

MEDICATIONS_PRESCRIBED:
  MEDICATION_1:
    GENERIC: Aspirin
    BRAND: Various
    PURPOSE: Reduces risk of future blood clots
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Low dose daily (81mg or 325mg)

  MEDICATION_2:
    GENERIC: Atorvastatin
    BRAND: Lipitor
    PURPOSE: Lowers cholesterol and stabilizes arterial plaque
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Varies
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Statin

  MEDICATION_3:
    GENERIC: Metoprolol
    BRAND: Lopressor
    PURPOSE: Beta blocker to reduce strain on the heart
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Varies
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Beta Blocker

UNDERWRITING_IMPACT:
  SEVERITY_LEVEL: Critical
  TYPICAL_OUTCOME: Decline or heavy table rating
  TIME_SINCE_EVENT: Critical factor - most carriers require 2-5+ years

CARRIER_POSITIONING:
  BOX_1_CARRIERS:
    LGA_OPTERM: Decline
    SBLI: Decline
    KANSAS_CITY_LIFE: Decline

  BOX_2_CARRIERS:
    MUTUAL_OF_OMAHA: Decline unless 2+ years and no complications

  BOX_3_CARRIERS:
    UHL_PRODUCTS: Decline or heavy rating

  BOX_4_CARRIERS:
    ELCO_PRODUCTS: Possible approval with rating depending on time and complications

  TRADITIONAL_CARRIERS:
    COREBRIDGE: Table rating possible if 5+ years, no complications
    TRANSAMERICA: Table rating possible if 5+ years, no complications

AGENT_GUIDANCE:
  RED_FLAGS: Recent MI (less than 2 years), multiple MIs, ongoing chest pain, stents, bypass surgery
  BEST_APPROACH: Wait until 2-5 years post-event, ensure stable condition, consider final expense products
  DOCUMENTATION_NEEDED: Cardiology records, stress test results, ejection fraction, current medications

SEVERITY: Major neurological event

MEDICATIONS_PRESCRIBED:
  MEDICATION_1:
    GENERIC: Clopidogrel
    BRAND: Plavix
    PURPOSE: Prevents blood clots
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Antiplatelet agent

  MEDICATION_2:
    GENERIC: Atorvastatin
    BRAND: Lipitor
    PURPOSE: Lowers cholesterol and reduces stroke recurrence
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Varies
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Statin

  MEDICATION_3:
    GENERIC: Warfarin
    BRAND: Coumadin
    PURPOSE: Anticoagulant for clot prevention
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Daily with INR monitoring
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Blood thinner
    ALTERNATIVE: Apixaban (Eliquis)

UNDERWRITING_IMPACT:
  SEVERITY_LEVEL: Critical
  TYPICAL_OUTCOME: Decline or heavy table rating
  TIME_SINCE_EVENT: Critical factor - most carriers require 5+ years for TIA only
  STROKE_VS_TIA: TIA (mini-stroke) has better underwriting outcomes than full stroke

CARRIER_POSITIONING:
  BOX_1_CARRIERS:
    ALL: Decline

  BOX_2_CARRIERS:
    ALL: Decline

  BOX_3_CARRIERS:
    ALL: Decline unless TIA only and 5+ years

  BOX_4_CARRIERS:
    ELCO_PRODUCTS: Possible consideration for TIA with significant time passed

  TRADITIONAL_CARRIERS:
    COREBRIDGE: Table rating possible if 5+ years TIA only, no recurrence
    TRANSAMERICA: Table rating possible if 5+ years TIA only, no recurrence

AGENT_GUIDANCE:
  RED_FLAGS: Full stroke vs TIA, residual deficits, multiple events, atrial fibrillation
  BEST_APPROACH: TIA only with 5+ years may have options, full stroke extremely limited
  DOCUMENTATION_NEEDED: Neurology records, imaging results, functional status, recurrence history

===CONDITION_3_DIABETES===
CONDITION_NAME: Diabetes Mellitus (Type 1 and Type 2)
MEDICAL_TERM: Diabetes, DM, Type 1 DM, Type 2 DM
SEVERITY: Chronic metabolic condition - severity varies by type and control

MEDICATIONS_PRESCRIBED:
  MEDICATION_1:
    GENERIC: Metformin
    BRAND: Glucophage
    PURPOSE: First-line oral medication for type 2 diabetes
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: 500mg to 2000mg daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Biguanide
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: Single oral medication suggests good control

  MEDICATION_2:
    GENERIC: Semaglutide
    BRAND: Ozempic
    PURPOSE: GLP-1 agonist for blood sugar control and weight loss
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Weekly injection
    MEDICATION_CLASS: GLP-1 receptor agonist
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: May indicate need for additional control beyond metformin

  MEDICATION_3:
    GENERIC: Insulin
    BRAND: Lantus, Humalog, Novolog
    PURPOSE: Injected hormone for blood sugar control
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Daily or multiple times daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Insulin
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: Insulin use indicates Type 1 or poorly controlled Type 2

  MEDICATION_4:
    GENERIC: Empagliflozin
    BRAND: Jardiance
    PURPOSE: SGLT2 inhibitor for blood sugar and heart protection
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: Daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: SGLT2 inhibitor
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: Often added for cardiovascular benefits

UNDERWRITING_IMPACT:
  SEVERITY_LEVEL: Moderate to Critical (depends on type and control)
  TYPE_1_DIABETES: Almost always decline or very heavy table rating
  TYPE_2_DIET_CONTROLLED: May qualify for Standard Plus or better at some carriers
  TYPE_2_SINGLE_ORAL_MED: Standard Plus or Standard with good A1C
  TYPE_2_INSULIN: Likely table rating or decline
  A1C_IMPORTANCE: Critical - under 7 is good, under 8 acceptable, over 8 problematic

CARRIER_POSITIONING:
  BOX_1_CARRIERS:
    LGA_OPTERM: Decline for any diabetes
    SBLI: Decline for any diabetes
    KANSAS_CITY_LIFE: Possible with diet-controlled only

  BOX_2_CARRIERS:
    MUTUAL_OF_OMAHA_IUL_EXPRESS: Accepts controlled diabetes BP cholesterol
    MUTUAL_OF_OMAHA_TERM_LIFE_EXPRESS: Accepts controlled diabetes BP cholesterol
    MUTUAL_OF_OMAHA_LIVING_PROMISE: Accepts controlled diabetes BP cholesterol

  BOX_3_CARRIERS:
    UHL_SIMPLE_TERM: Accepts diabetes and heart issues
    UHL_PROVIDER_WHOLE_LIFE: Moderate health issues accepted
    UHL_EXPRESS_ISSUE: Multiple health tiers available
    UHL_FINAL_EXPENSE: Lenient underwriting

  BOX_4_CARRIERS:
    ELCO_GOLDEN_EAGLE: Best rate approve everyone
    ELCO_SILVER_EAGLE: Multiple health tiers
    ELCO_PRESIDIO_IET: Guaranteed issue
    ELCO_PRESIDIO_IFT: Guaranteed issue

  TRADITIONAL_CARRIERS:
    COREBRIDGE: Standard Plus possible if diet controlled or single oral med with A1C under 7
    TRANSAMERICA: Standard Plus possible if diet controlled or single oral med with A1C under 7

AGENT_GUIDANCE:
  MEDICATION_COMBINATION_SEVERITY:
    METFORMIN_ONLY: Good control, Box 2 likely
    METFORMIN_PLUS_OZEMPIC_OR_JARDIANCE: Moderate control, Box 2 or 3
    INSULIN_USE: Poor control or Type 1, Box 3 or 4 only
  QUESTIONS_TO_ASK:
    - Type 1 or Type 2?
    - When diagnosed?
    - Most recent A1C level?
    - Any complications (neuropathy, retinopathy, kidney issues)?
  BEST_APPROACH: Box 2 carriers (Mutual of Omaha) specifically mention accepting controlled diabetes
  DOCUMENTATION_NEEDED: A1C results, medication list, complication status, physician notes

MEDICAL_TERM: Hypertension, HTN, Essential Hypertension
SEVERITY: Mild to Moderate (very common, usually manageable)

MEDICATIONS_PRESCRIBED:

    PURPOSE: ACE inhibitor to lower blood pressure
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: 5mg to 40mg daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: ACE Inhibitor
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: Single medication suggests good control

  MEDICATION_2:
    GENERIC: Amlodipine
    BRAND: Norvasc
    PURPOSE: Calcium channel blocker to relax blood vessels
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: 2.5mg to 10mg daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: Calcium Channel Blocker
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: Often first-line or added to ACE inhibitor

  MEDICATION_3:
    GENERIC: Losartan
    BRAND: Cozaar
    PURPOSE: ARB (angiotensin receptor blocker) to lower blood pressure
    DOSAGE_INDICATOR: 25mg to 100mg daily
    MEDICATION_CLASS: ARB
    SEVERITY_INDICATOR: Alternative to ACE inhibitors

UNDERWRITING_IMPACT:
  SEVERITY_LEVEL: Mild to Moderate
  SINGLE_MEDICATION_CONTROLLED: Usually acceptable at most carriers
  MULTIPLE_MEDICATIONS: May result in rating depending on control
  BLOOD_PRESSURE_READINGS: More important than medication - controlled BP is key

CARRIER_POSITIONING:
  BOX_1_CARRIERS:
    LGA_OPTERM: Preferred Plus if 140/90 or lower untreated, Preferred if 145/95 or lower
    SBLI: Similar to LGA standards
    KANSAS_CITY_LIFE: Moderate health accepted

  BOX_2_CARRIERS:
    MUTUAL_OF_OMAHA_ALL: Accepts controlled diabetes BP cholesterol

  BOX_3_CARRIERS:
    UHL_ALL: Very lenient

  BOX_4_CARRIERS:
    ELCO_ALL: Best rate approve everyone

  TRADITIONAL_CARRIERS:
    COREBRIDGE: Multiple rate classes based on BP readings and medication count
    TRANSAMERICA: Preferred Plus 140/90 untreated, Preferred 145/95, Standard Plus 150/100

AGENT_GUIDANCE:
  MEDICATION_COUNT_MATTERS:
    ONE_MEDICATION: Usually Standard or better if controlled
    TWO_MEDICATIONS: Standard Plus or Standard
    THREE_PLUS_MEDICATIONS: Likely table rating
  QUESTIONS_TO_ASK:
    - What are your typical BP readings at home?
    - How long on medication?
    - Any recent changes to medications?
  BEST_APPROACH: Controlled BP on 1-2 medications works at almost all carriers
  DOCUMENTATION_NEEDED: Recent BP readings, medication list, physician notes

    - Full recovery expected?
    - Any complications?

  DOCUMENTATION_NEEDED: Operative report, pathology (if applicable), recovery status

===QUICK_CARRIER_SELECTOR_GUIDE===
PURPOSE: Fast reference for medication-based carrier positioning

SCENARIO: Client on single BP or cholesterol medication, controlled
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Box 1: LGA, SBLI, Kansas City Life (best rates)
  - Box 2: Mutual of Omaha (good rates, mild health accepted)

SCENARIO: Client on diabetes medication (metformin or similar), controlled
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Box 2: Mutual of Omaha (specifically accepts controlled diabetes)
  - Box 3: UHL (lenient)
  - Box 4: ELCO (best rates approve everyone)

SCENARIO: Client on diabetes medication PLUS BP medication
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Box 2: Mutual of Omaha (accepts controlled diabetes BP cholesterol)
  - Box 3: UHL (very lenient)
  - Box 4: ELCO (best rates approve everyone)

SCENARIO: Client on insulin for diabetes
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Box 3: UHL (accepts diabetes and health issues)
  - Box 4: ELCO (best rates approve everyone)
  - Consider: ELCO guaranteed issue products

SCENARIO: Client on heart attack medications (aspirin, statin, beta blocker)
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Time critical: Need 2-5+ years post-event
  - Box 4: ELCO (possible with rating)
  - Traditional: Corebridge, Transamerica (5+ years, table rating)

SCENARIO: Client on stroke medications (Plavix, blood thinners)
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Very limited options
  - TIA only with 5+ years: Some carriers may consider
  - Full stroke: Extremely limited, consider final expense

SCENARIO: Client on psychiatric medications (SSRI, antidepressants)
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Mild, single medication, stable: Box 2 and up
  - Box 2: Mutual of Omaha
  - Box 3: UHL
  - Box 4: ELCO

SCENARIO: Client on bipolar medications (Lithium, Seroquel)
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Very limited options
  - Box 4: ELCO guaranteed issue (Presidio IFT/IET)
  - Most carriers: Decline

SCENARIO: Client on asthma medications (inhalers)
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - Mild/occasional use: Box 1 possible
  - Daily maintenance inhalers: Box 2 and up
  - Severe: Box 4 ELCO

SCENARIO: Client on thyroid medication (Synthroid/Levothyroxine)
RECOMMENDED_CARRIERS:
  - All boxes acceptable
  - Minimal underwriting impact
  - Works everywhere if controlled

===COMMON_MEDICATION_COMBINATIONS===
COMBINATION: Metformin + Lisinopril + Atorvastatin
INDICATES: Type 2 Diabetes + High Blood Pressure + High Cholesterol (Metabolic Syndrome)
SEVERITY: Moderate - multiple conditions but commonly managed together
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Box 2 (Mutual of Omaha specifically accepts "controlled diabetes BP cholesterol")
ALTERNATE: Box 3 (UHL) or Box 4 (ELCO)

COMBINATION: Aspirin + Atorvastatin + Metoprolol + Lisinopril
INDICATES: Post-Heart Attack with ongoing cardiac care
SEVERITY: Critical - major cardiac history
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Very limited options, likely decline at most carriers
TIME_CRITICAL: Need 2-5+ years since event
POSSIBLE_CARRIERS: ELCO with rating, Corebridge/Transamerica if 5+ years

COMBINATION: Levothyroxine + Sertraline
INDICATES: Hypothyroidism + Depression or Anxiety
SEVERITY: Mild to Moderate - both conditions commonly managed
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Box 2 or above
NOTES: Thyroid minimal impact, focus on depression/anxiety history

COMBINATION: Albuterol + Fluticasone/Advair
INDICATES: Asthma requiring daily maintenance medication
SEVERITY: Moderate - daily medication indicates chronic condition
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Box 2 if stable, Box 3 if moderate severity
QUESTIONS: Hospitalization history critical

COMBINATION: Ozempic + Metformin + Jardiance
INDICATES: Type 2 Diabetes requiring multiple medications for control
SEVERITY: Moderate to Severe - three medications suggests difficulty controlling
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Box 3 (UHL) or Box 4 (ELCO)
AVOID: Box 1 carriers will decline

COMBINATION: Insulin (any type)
INDICATES: Type 1 Diabetes OR poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes
SEVERITY: Severe
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Box 3 (UHL) or Box 4 (ELCO) only
NOTES: Insulin use is major underwriting concern, Box 1 and 2 likely decline

COMBINATION: Lithium + Quetiapine (Seroquel)
INDICATES: Bipolar Disorder
SEVERITY: Critical
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Box 4 guaranteed issue only (ELCO Presidio IFT/IET)
NOTES: Most carriers will decline, very limited options

COMBINATION: Warfarin or Eliquis (Apixaban)
INDICATES: Atrial Fibrillation, Stroke history, or other clotting disorder
SEVERITY: Moderate to Critical depending on underlying condition
RECOMMENDED_BOX: Depends on reason for anticoagulation
NOTES: Need to determine WHY on blood thinners - critical question
